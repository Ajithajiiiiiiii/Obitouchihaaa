Education

Private schools in Delhi—which use either English or Hindi as the language of instruction—are affiliated to one of three administering bodies, the Council for the Indian School Certificate Examinations (CISCE), the Central Board for Secondary Education (CBSE)[249] or the National Institute of Open Schooling (NIOS). In 2004–05, approximately 1,529,000 students were enrolled in primary schools, 822,000 in middle schools and 669,000 in secondary schools across Delhi.[250] Female students represented 49% of the total enrolment. The same year, the Delhi government spent between 1.58% and 1.95% of its gross state domestic product on education.[250]

Delhi Technological University (formerly Delhi College of Engineering), Indira Gandhi Delhi Technical University for Women (formerly Indira Gandhi Institute of Technology), Indraprastha Institute of Information Technology, Netaji Subhas University of Technology (formerly Netaji Subhas Institute of Technology), Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University and National Law University, Delhi are the only state universities.[253][254] University of Delhi, Jawaharlal Nehru University and Jamia Millia Islamia are the central universities, and Indira Gandhi National Open University is for distance education.[255] As of 2008, about 16% of all Delhi residents possessed at least a college graduate degree.

Schools and higher educational institutions in Delhi are administered either by the Directorate of Education, the NCT government or private organisations. In 2006, Delhi had 165 colleges, five medical colleges and eight engineering colleges,[250] seven major universities and nine deemed universities.

The premier management colleges of Delhi such as Faculty of Management Studies (Delhi) and Indian Institute of Foreign Trade rank the best in India.

Indraprastha College for Women, established in 1924

Founded in 1924, Indraprastha College for Women stands as a pioneering institution in Delhi's educational landscape. Affiliated with the University of Delhi, it provides diverse academic programs and fosters holistic development. With a rich legacy, it continues to empower women through quality education, innovation, and a commitment to excellence.

Jamia Millia Islamia est. 1920 by M. A. Ansari and Zakir Husain

Established in 1920 by visionary leaders M. A. Ansari and Zakir Husain, Jamia Millia Islamia in Delhi is a prominent educational institution. It promotes inclusivity, academic excellence, and cultural diversity, offering a range of programs that reflect its commitment to fostering knowledge and social responsibility.

University of Delhi was founded in 1922. Sir Maurice Gwyer served as its first vice-chancellor.

Established in 1922, the University of Delhi, led by its first vice-chancellor Sir Maurice Gwyer, has been a cornerstone of higher education in India. Renowned for academic excellence and diverse disciplines, it continues to shape generations of scholars and contribute significantly to India's educational landscape.

Dormitory of Anglo Arabic Senior Secondary School, founded 1696, reorganised 1828

The dormitory of Anglo Arabic Senior Secondary School, founded in 1696 and reorganized in 1828, holds a unique historical legacy. Nestled in Delhi, it represents a blend of centuries-old heritage and modern education. The school continues to shape young minds, preserving cultural roots while fostering academic growth and global perspectives.
