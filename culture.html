Festivals

Delhi, as the capital of India, is rich in cultural diversity, and various festivals are celebrated with enthusiasm throughout the year. Some of the prominent festivals and cultural events in Delhi include:

Diwali

The festival of lights, Diwali, is celebrated with grandeur. Homes and streets are adorned with colorful lights, and firecrackers light up the night sky

Holi

Known as the festival of colors, Holi is celebrated with playful abandon. People come together to smear each other with vibrant colored powders and enjoy festive meals.

Eid-ul-Fitr and Eid-ul-adha

Delhi's significant Muslim population celebrates these two major Islamic festivals with prayers, feasts, and social gatherings.

Durga pooja

Bengali community in Delhi celebrates Durga Puja with elaborate decorations, cultural programs, and processions.

Christmas

The Christian community celebrates Christmas with midnight masses, decorations, and festive events

Navratri and Dushera

These Hindu festivals are celebrated with traditional dance forms like Garba during Navratri and the burning of effigies on Dussehra symbolizing the triumph of good over evil.

Maha Shivaratri

Maha Shivaratri is a Hindu festival dedicated to Lord Shiva, one of the principal deities in Hinduism. It usually falls in the month of February or March. Devotees observe fasts, visit Shiva temples, and offer prayers to seek Lord Shiva's blessings.

Janmashtami

Janmashtami celebrates the birth of Lord Krishna, another significant deity in Hinduism. It usually occurs in August or September. Devotees fast, sing devotional songs, and participate in dance-dramas reenacting episodes from Krishna's life.
